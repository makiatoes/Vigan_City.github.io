Calle Crisologo

Walking along the cobbled stones of Calle Crisologo brings back the mood of the historic Spanish times within the serenity of old ancestral houses built by Chinese mestizos (Fil-Chinese); an excellent place to shop for souvenir items, antiques and furniture. Lies along the streets of Calle Crisologo are hotels, restaurants and coffee shops. It is the best place to ride a Calesa.

Villa Angela

Villa Angela Heritage House located along Quirino Blvd., is a typical ancestral house constructed in Vigan in the 1800s. Agapito B. Florendo, a gobernadorcillo, had it built in 1870. The house itself is part of a 2,000 square meter property owned by the prominent Versoza family. They named it after their matriarch, Angela Versoza VilÂ¬lanueva. The property was restored by descendants in the eighties. It has since then become a depository of the mementos acquired by the family from the earliest years onwards, which they proudly share with their visitors

Quema House

The Quema House is the ancestral house of the Quema Family. Built in 1820, the house is a great example of a traditional bahay-na-bato style that was popular among the Philippine mixed-race era.
