National Museum Ilocos Complex

The museum which is managed by the National Museum houses paintings of Esteban Pichay Villanueva of the 1807 Basi Revolt. It also exhibits the "basi", the quintessential Ilocano liquor made of fermented sugar-cane juice. Also found in the museum are paintings of National Artist Fernando Amorsolo and a bust by National Artist Guillermo Tolentino. The museum was used to be the Provincial Jail where President Elpidio Quirino was born.

Fr. Jose Burgos National Museum

The Fr. Jose Burgos Museum is an excellent showcase of archeological and ethnographic treasures, antiques, dioramas of local historical events and photographs of Ilocano heroes and achievers. It is the birthplace of Fr. Jose Apolonio Burgos, one of the most illustrious sons of Vigan who fought for ecclesiastical reforms during the Spanish colonial period.

Crisologo Museum

Also known as the Vigan House National Historical Institute. The museum consists of the preserved ancestral home of statesman Floro S. Crisologo and houses the family's memorabilia and private collection of religious figures as well as some permanent exhibits on Northern Luzon's history, ethnography, archaeology, and natural history.

Vigan Conservation Complex

This complex houses three museums, a repository of archives, a conservation school and a dormitory. The three museums revolve around the concepts of the city's Pammati ken Kari (Devotion and Power), Cuidad Lungsod (Outlining the city) and Casa Bigueno (the evolution of the Vigan House).

San Pablo Museum

The Museo San Pablo found inside the Conversion of St. Paul Metropolitan Cathedral is a good place for devotees to see old "Santos" (religious statues) and aged collection of photos by a German pharmacist who lived in Vigan in the late 1800's.

Hotel Luna

Hotel Luna, the first and only Museum Hotel in the Philippines named after the celebrated Ilocano painter Juan Luna. A luxury hotel that houses different antiquities, contemporary arts and artworks by national artist such as Jose Joya, Abdulmari Asia Imao (who also did the Luna bust), Bencab, H.R. Ocampo, Napoleon Abueva, Guillermo Tolentino, Vicente Manansala, Juvenal Sanso and Carlos "Botong" Francisco are displayed in the hotel. Formally opened to the public last 2012.

Arzobispado Palacio de Nueva Segovia

Built in 1783 and the only surviving 18th century Arzobispado in the country. The Arzobispado of Vigan is the official residence of the Archbishop of Nueva Segovia. It served as the headquarters of Gen. Manuel Tinio in 1898 and the invading American forces under Col. James Parker in 1899. Its Museo Nueva Segovia showcases ecclesiastical artifacts, antique portraits of bishops, a throne room, archdiocesan and other religious articles gathered from various colonial churches all over 'locos Sur.
