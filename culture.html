Pagburnayan (Jar Factory)

A pre-colonial product of Vigan and one of the traditional industries of the city. Here, guests will be encouraged to try their skills in jar making using centuries old foot-driven potter's wheel. Have a chance to meet one of the National Folk Artist Awardee for Pottery, Mr. Fidel Go at Ruby Pottery.

Able Loom Weaving

Observe the process and experience the making of abel-iloco, another pre-colonial product of Vigan and one of the major exports during the Galleon trade.

Damili (Terracotta)

The Ilocano term for molding. This terra-cotta or red-clay craft is one of the traditional industries that Vigan is known for. It has existed since the 19th century and is still done in homes within several of Vigan's barangays. Local and foreign visitors who wish to see how this craft is done may visit the barangays of Ayusan Sur, Barraca, Bulala, Paratong, Paoa, Pong-ol and Salindeg. Of these barangays, Bulala is most known for damili-making and, therefore, most visited.

Kankanen Village

Aside from old Spanish Structures, Vigan is also known for its delicacies. The Kankanen Village is where we find the Kankanen makers of Bananaque, Kamoteque, Butchique, Donutque, Kaskaron, Patupat, Sinuman, Biko-Biko, Dila-Dila and the like. These delicacies are best pasalubong from Vigan to take home for friends and families.
